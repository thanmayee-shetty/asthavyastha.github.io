1. Food for Homeless and Nutrition

0. Indicator Information (SDG_INDICATOR_INFO)
0.a. Goal (SDG_GOAL)
Goal 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
0.b. Target (SDG_TARGET) Target 11.1: By 2030, make sure cities and human settlements are inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
0.c. Indicator (SDG_INDICATOR) Indicator 11.1.2: Proportion of population living in slums, informal settlements, or in inadequate housing.

2.a. Algorithmic Applications:
Food Distribution Route Optimization (Dijkstra's, BFS, Heap):
Dijkstra's Algorithm: To find the shortest and most efficient food delivery routes, so that resources reach the vulnerable areas with the fastest speed.
BFS: For finding the shortest path in an unweighted grid (urban streets), especially for emergency deliveries.
Heap: For managing priority queues in food distribution logistics, where the most urgent areas (e.g., shelters) get resources first.

Cost-Efficient Food Allocation (Kruskal’s, Prim’s, Union-Find, Segment Tree):
Kruskal’s/Prim’s Algorithms: To design cost-effective networks for food distribution, minimizing the overall transportation costs.
Union-Find: To handle connected components and maintain different segments of the distribution network.
Segment Tree: For fast queries and updates about the status of food distribution (for instance, the number of stocks, transport times).

Food Demand Forecasting (Binary Search Tree, AVL Tree, Trie):
BST: For fast operations of insertion and search operations on food demand data for many regions.
AVL Tree: For balancing demand data to be inserted/deleted or searched quickly in terms of food requirements.
Trie: For location-based food requests for storing them in terms of prefixes, and easily spotting food needs' patterns.

2. Drainage Systems

0. Indicator Information (SDG_INDICATOR_INFO)
Goal (SDG_GOAL): Goal 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
Target (SDG_TARGET): Target 11.5: By 2030, significantly reduce the number of deaths and the number of people affected and substantially decrease the direct economic losses relative to global GDP caused by disasters, including water-related disasters.
Indicator (SDG_INDICATOR): Indicator 11.5.2: Direct economic loss attributed to disasters, including water-related disasters.

2.a. Algorithmic Applications:
Efficient Drainage Design (Dijkstra's, BFS, Kruskal's, Prim's, Segment Tree):
Dijkstra's Algorithm: In designing shortest paths between drainage points to ensure optimal water flow.
BFS: To traverse all the possible drainage routes and ensure that all points are joined with efficiency in an urban grid.
Kruskal's/Prim's: For forming the MST of drainage systems with minimum cost and covering all parts of the city.
Segment Tree: Use for querying the status of a particular drainage system segment, such as water levels and flow rates in specific parts of the city.

Flood Prediction and Water Management (Floyd-Warshall, Fenwick Tree, DFS):
Floyd-Warshall: Used in determining the shortest path from many drainage nodes within a city to predict the areas which could flood.
Fenwick Tree: In handling queries and updates on the water levels over drainage segments efficiently.
DFS: For searching drainage networks and tracking the presence of obstructions or flaws in the water flow.

Resource Allocation and Management (Heap, Union-Find):
Heap: Areas needing immediate repair of drainage. Sorting the areas based on urgency of drainage repair.
Union-Find: To determine interconnected drainage systems and their status in order to ensure timely fixing.

3. Disaster Risk Management

0. Indicator Information (SDG_INDICATOR_INFO)
Goal (SDG_GOAL): Goal 11: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
Target (SDG_TARGET): Target 11.5: By 2030, significantly reduce the number of deaths and the number of people affected and substantially decrease the direct economic losses relative to global GDP caused by disasters, including water-related disasters.
Indicator (SDG_INDICATOR): Indicator 11.5.1: Number of deaths, missing persons, and directly affected persons attributed to disasters.

2.a. Algorithmic Applications:
BFS: Determine the safest evacuation route to the shortest to ensure speedy evacuation of citizens.
DFS: For mapping of the evacuation network, thereby ensuring all routes are explored safely.
Heap/Priority Queue: In the case of priority evacuation of the most vulnerable members.

Resource Distribution (Dijkstra's, Kruskal's, Prim's, BFS, Union-Find):
Dijkstra's Algorithm: In the process of emergency supply routing in terms of food, water, medicines, and the like toward disaster-hit regions.
Kruskal’s/Prim’s: To build cost-effective resource distribution networks that minimize transportation delays.
BFS: For ensuring that all affected areas are reached by emergency teams, especially in urban grids.
Union-Find: For managing and connecting affected regions in the disaster response network.

Damage Assessment and Recovery (Kruskal’s, AVL Tree, Red-Black Tree, Heap):
Kruskal’s Algorithm: For finding the optimal network for damage inspection teams to efficiently cover the most affected areas.
AVL Tree/Red-Black Tree: In order to manage dynamic data on infrastructure damage, with quick search and update operations.
Heap: To prioritize repair teams based on the severity of damage in different parts of the city.

Flood Prediction (Warshall's, Segment Tree, DFS):
Warshall's Algorithm: To calculate potential flood paths and identify weak points in the city's drainage system.
Segment Tree: In order to handle water level updates and queries efficiently over different flood-prone zones.
DFS: Explore possible flood paths and areas that require urgent intervention.
